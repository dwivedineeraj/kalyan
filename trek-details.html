Pikey Peak Trekking - 9 Days

Solukhumbu Region 9 Days Max Alt: 4,065m Grade: Moderate (Based on reviews)

Trip Overview

Pikey Peak Trekking is a newly discovered route in the lower Everest region, famed for offering perhaps the best viewpoint of Mount Everest in Nepal, as acclaimed by Sir Edmund Hillary himself. This 9-day trek presents a unique opportunity to witness breathtaking sunrises and sunsets over the Himalayan giants, including Everest, Makalu, Kanchenjunga, Annapurna, and Dhaulagiri, from the summit of Pikey Peak (4,065m). Unlike the more crowded trails to Everest Base Camp, the Pikey Peak trek meanders through serene landscapes, traditional Sherpa villages, ancient monasteries, and lush rhododendron forests, providing an authentic cultural experience and stunning natural beauty away from the main tourist throngs.

Ideal for those with limited time or seeking a less strenuous alternative to high-altitude treks, the Pikey Peak journey combines moderate hiking with unparalleled panoramic views. The trek is accessible, requiring no prior trekking experience, and offers comfortable teahouse accommodation along the way. Experience the tranquility of the lower Khumbu, interact with friendly locals, and capture unforgettable Himalayan vistas on this rewarding adventure.

Detailed Itinerary

Day 01: Arrival in Kathmandu (1,400 m) & Transfer to Hotel

Upon arrival, transfer to your hotel. Briefing about the trek in the evening. Overnight in Kathmandu.

Day 02: Drive Kathmandu to Dhap (2,900 m) (7-8 hrs drive)

Scenic drive through hills and river valleys to reach Dhap. Overnight at a teahouse.

Day 03: Trek Dhap to Jhapre (2,920 m) (4-5 hrs trek)

Trek through forests and small villages with views emerging. Visit a local monastery at Jhapre. Overnight at a teahouse.

Day 04: Trek Jhapre to Pikey Base Camp (3,640 m) (5-6 hrs trek)

Ascend towards Pikey Base Camp, passing yak pastures and stone mani walls. Enjoy growing mountain vistas. Overnight at a teahouse.

Day 05: Hike to Pikey Peak (4,065 m), Trek to Loding (2,515 m) (6-7 hrs trek)

Early morning hike to Pikey Peak for sunrise views over Everest, Makalu, Kanchenjunga, etc. Descend through forest to Loding. Overnight at a teahouse.

Day 06: Trek Loding to Junbesi (2,700 m), Visit Thubtenchoeling (4-5 hrs trek)

Trek down to Junbesi, a traditional Sherpa village. Optional visit to the large Thubtenchoeling Monastery nearby. Overnight at a teahouse.

Day 07: Explore Junbesi Village

Acclimatization or rest day. Explore the village, visit the local school, or hike to nearby viewpoints. Overnight at a teahouse.

Day 08: Trek Junbesi to Phaplu (2,470 m) (3-4 hrs trek)

A relatively short trek down to Phaplu, the district headquarters with an airstrip. Overnight at a teahouse.

Day 09: Drive Phaplu to Kathmandu (8-9 hrs drive)

Long drive back to Kathmandu. Farewell dinner in the evening. Overnight in Kathmandu.

Note: Itinerary can be customized based on preferences and time.

Essential Information

Permits Required

You will need the Gaurishankar Conservation Area Permit (GCAP) and a Trekkers' Information Management System (TIMS) card. We will obtain these permits for you as part of the package.

Best Time to Trek

The best seasons are Spring (March to May) and Autumn (September to November). Spring offers blooming rhododendrons and mild weather. Autumn provides clear skies and stable conditions. Winter is cold, and monsoon brings rain and leeches.

Accommodation and Food

Accommodation during the trek is in basic teahouses or lodges. Rooms are usually twin-sharing with shared bathrooms. Meals provided are typically Nepali Dal Bhat, noodles, rice dishes, soups, and basic breakfast items. You can buy snacks and drinks along the way.

Travel Insurance

Comprehensive travel insurance is mandatory for this trek. It must cover emergency medical evacuation (including helicopter rescue) up to at least 5000 meters, trip cancellation, and medical expenses.

Guide and Porter

An experienced, government-licensed guide will lead your trek, ensuring safety and providing local insights. Porters will carry your main duffel bag (limited weight, usually 10-12 kg per trekker).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How high is Pikey Peak?

Pikey Peak stands at 4,065 meters (13,337 feet) above sea level.

Can you see Everest from Pikey Peak?

Yes, Pikey Peak offers one of the best panoramic views of Mount Everest and many other Himalayan giants, including Makalu, Kanchenjunga, Lhotse, Annapurna, and Dhaulagiri ranges.

Is the Pikey Peak trek difficult?

The trek is graded as moderate. It involves daily walking of 4-7 hours on undulating trails. While it doesn't reach extreme altitudes like Everest Base Camp, a good level of basic fitness is required. The highest point is Pikey Peak itself at 4,065m.

Do I need the Lukla flight for this trek?

No, a major advantage of the Pikey Peak trek is that it avoids the often unreliable Lukla flight. You travel overland by road (typically shared jeep or bus) from Kathmandu to the trek start point (Dhap) and return from the end point (Phaplu).

Is Pikey Peak trek suitable for beginners or families?

Yes, this trek can be suitable for adventurous beginners and families with older children who have a good level of fitness and enjoy hiking. It offers incredible Himalayan views without the extreme altitude and difficulty of longer treks.

How is the network and charging availability on the Pikey Peak route?

Mobile network coverage (NTC/Ncell) can be patchy, available mainly near villages but often not on the higher trails. Charging facilities are usually available at teahouses, often for a small fee.
